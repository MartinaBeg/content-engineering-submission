## The five messaging pillars

| # | Pillar | Claim | Mechanism | Anchor proof |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P1** | **Finance-grade, by design** | Built to finance's bar for accuracy, control, and audit defensibility — not generic AI repurposed for finance. | ISO/IEC 42001 certified; operates inside the same controls and audit frameworks Zuora customers already trust. | "No Zuora public company has ever failed a revenue audit." |
| **P2** | **Embedded, not bolted on** | Lives inside the system of record. No data movement, no shadow audit trail, no integration tax. | Inherits the permissions, audit trail, and approval policies of Zuora's quote-to-cash platform. | 53% of finance leaders trust AI embedded in existing solutions most — vs. 25% in-house, 13% AI-native (Harris Poll, 2026). |
| **P3** | **Explainable and defensible** | Every output exposes its math — SSP, ASC 606 formulas, allocation deltas, calculation traces. | Side-by-side allocation comparison; reviewable in a sandbox; defensible to an auditor. | 33% of finance leaders name the inability to audit or explain AI outputs as the biggest gap in finance AI today. |
| **P4** | **Human-in-the-loop, always** | AI proposes. Finance disposes. Simulation is the default; commit follows existing approval policies. | Configurable approval gates by role and amount; inherited permissions. | 38% of finance leaders name lack of human oversight as a top AI concern. |
| **P5** | **Productive at finance scale** | Reclaims hours per week from contract triage, audit response, write-offs, cash app, and collections. | Embedded agents across billing, collections, and revenue recognition. | ZOLL Data Systems deployed Zuora AI-Powered Collections in four weeks — payment recovery up, DSO down, manual work down. |
